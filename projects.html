Mechatronics 01

Custom Mini-Z 1:28 RC Platform

ATtiny85 DRV8833 KiCad Fusion 360
Interactive 3D Model — drag to rotate, scroll to zoom

Place your miniZ.glb in models/

File: models/miniZ.glb

Problem

At 1:28 scale, the available volume for electronics and mounting hardware is extremely limited. Off-the-shelf RC controllers could not fit the vehicle layout, which required a compact solution for steering, drive, and power delivery.

Solution

Developed a custom controller PCB and 3D-printed chassis that fit the vehicle envelope while supporting motor and steering control. The board uses an ATtiny85 and DRV8833 dual H-bridge, with an AMS1117-5.0 regulator for a stable 5 V rail. PCB and chassis features were designed together to ensure fit, mounting, and wiring clearance.

Tools & Process

  • KiCad — Schematic capture, PCB layout, and board outline definition
  • Fusion 360 — Chassis CAD, tolerance analysis, and STEP export for fit verification
  • FDM 3D Printing — Rapid prototyping of structural and mounting components
  • Arduino C++ — ATtiny85 firmware for motor drive and servo control (programmed using an Arduino UNO as ISP)
  • Reflow / Hand Soldering — SMD population and bring-up validation

Embedded Systems 02

Centralized Vehicle Control Unit (CVCU)

ESP32-WROOM RFID MOSFET Switching KiCad
Interactive 3D Model — drag to rotate, scroll to zoom

Place your powerWheels.glb in models/

File: models/powerWheels.glb

Problem

Ride-on vehicle controls were split into separate consumer modules, which made sensing, access control, and power switching unreliable at the system level. The design needed a central board that could handle high motor current while providing a cleaner user interface and secure start control.

Solution

Built a custom 2-layer central control PCB for a Power Wheels platform. It uses an ESP32-WROOM-32U for control and telemetry, RFID for access control, OLED for status display, and a DFR0299 amplifier for audio feedback. AO3400 MOSFETs switch the motor current, while an SPDT relay isolates main power. The board was assembled with heavy-duty terminals for safe motor connections and integrated firmware, schematic, and layout work into one system.

Tools & Process

  • KiCad — Schematic capture, PCB layout, and design rule checking
  • ESP32 / Arduino — Firmware development, peripheral integration, and telemetry
  • Reflow Heat Plate — Controlled SMD reflow for component population
  • Precision Soldering — Hand assembly of connectors, relays, and power-stage components
  • Oscilloscope / DMM — Power rail validation and switching verification
