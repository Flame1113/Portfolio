Mechatronics 01

Custom Mini-Z 1:28 RC Platform

ATtiny85 DRV8833 KiCad Fusion 360
Interactive 3D Model — drag to rotate, scroll to zoom

Place your miniZ.glb in models/

File: models/miniZ.glb

Problem

At 1:28 scale, RC platforms impose severe packaging constraints on both mechanical structure and onboard electronics. Commercial controller boards and chassis assemblies are not co-designed for custom geometries, leaving no viable path to integrate a bespoke drivetrain, steering servo, and power distribution within sub-millimeter clearances.

Solution

Engineered a fully integrated mechatronic platform pairing a custom 3D-printed chassis with a purpose-built controller PCB. The board centers on an ATtiny85-20P microcontroller and a DRV8833PWP dual H-bridge motor driver IC for coordinated propulsion and steering servo actuation, with an AMS1117-5.0 LDO supplying a regulated 5 V rail. PCB outline, mounting features, and chassis geometry were developed concurrently so electrical and mechanical subsystems share the same dimensional envelope.

Tools & Process

  • KiCad — Schematic capture, PCB layout, and board outline definition
  • Fusion 360 — Chassis CAD, tolerance analysis, and STEP export for fit verification
  • FDM 3D Printing — Rapid prototyping of structural and mounting components
  • Arduino C++ — ATtiny85 firmware for motor drive and servo control
  • Reflow / Hand Soldering — SMD population and bring-up validation

Embedded Systems 02

Centralized Vehicle Control Unit (CVCU)

ESP32-WROOM RFID MOSFET Switching KiCad
Interactive 3D Model — drag to rotate, scroll to zoom

Place your motor-esc.glb in models/

File: models/motor-esc.glb

Problem

Stock ride-on vehicle electronics distribute control across discrete consumer modules with no unified I/O, access control, or telemetry path. High-current 550-size motor loads require robust switching and isolation, while user-facing subsystems — status display, audio feedback, and wireless connectivity — must coexist on a single board without compromising propulsion reliability.

Solution

Designed a custom 2-layer PCB as the centralized vehicle control unit for a Power Wheels platform. An ESP32-WROOM-32U provides firmware execution and IoT telemetry; an RFID module implements access control; a monochrome OLED delivers onboard status UI; and a DFR0299 audio amplifier IC drives integrated audio output. Propulsion is switched through AO3400 N-channel MOSFETs, with an SPDT mechanical relay providing main power isolation. Heavy-duty screw terminals route high-current motor connections off-board. Firmware, schematic design, layout, and board assembly were executed end-to-end as a single integrated system.

Tools & Process

  • KiCad — Schematic capture, PCB layout, and design rule checking
  • ESP32 / Arduino — Firmware development, peripheral integration, and telemetry
  • Reflow Heat Plate — Controlled SMD reflow for component population
  • Precision Soldering — Hand assembly of connectors, relays, and power-stage components
  • Oscilloscope / DMM — Power rail validation and switching verification
